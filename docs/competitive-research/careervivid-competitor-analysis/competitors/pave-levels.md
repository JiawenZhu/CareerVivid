# Pave / Levels.fyi — Competitor Profile

> **Threat Level: 🟢 LOW**
> **Category fit:** Different — compensation data / salary benchmarking
> **XPRIZE risk:** Different market focus, B2B compensation tools, not job-seeker AI tools

---

## Overview
Pave (compensation management for companies) and Levels.fyi (crowdsourced comp data for tech job seekers) serve adjacent but distinct markets. Levels.fyi has 2M+ users who use it to benchmark tech salaries (total compensation, stock breakdowns, offer letters). Pave serves HR teams at companies to set and manage compensation bands. Neither is primarily a job-seeking AI tool.

## Products
| Company | Product | Users | Revenue Model |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Levels.fyi** | Crowdsourced tech comp database | 2M+ | B2B data licensing + job board |
| **Pave** | Compensation management SaaS | Enterprise | $24K–$240K/yr per company |

## Why They're Low Threat
1. **Different primary user.** Levels.fyi is a research tool, not a job application tool. Users visit once per offer negotiation, not daily.
2. **B2B focus (Pave).** Pave sells to HR teams, not job seekers. They're in a completely different category.
3. **No AI writing/application features.** Neither product helps you write a resume, submit applications, or prep for interviews.
4. **Tech sector narrow focus.** Levels.fyi is almost exclusively software engineering / tech. CareerVivid serves all industries.

## Potential Overlap
- Salary data in CareerVivid's Chrome extension (currently available) overlaps with Levels.fyi's data. If a user sees a salary extracted from a job posting, they might cross-reference Levels.fyi.
- CareerVivid could partner with or license Levels.fyi data rather than competing.

## XPRIZE Assessment
Pave and Levels.fyi are not XPRIZE competitors in CareerVivid's category. Pave is enterprise HR software; Levels.fyi is a data resource site. Neither would enter "Entrepreneurship & Job Creation" as a job-seeker AI platform.

## Opportunity
- If CareerVivid could surface Levels.fyi-style compensation benchmarks natively in the job tracker ("This role pays P75 $180K total comp based on 340 data points from similar roles"), it would be a feature that directly differentiates from every pure resume tool in this analysis.
